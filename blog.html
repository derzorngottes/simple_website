A Deep Learning Dream

February 15th, 2016 by Amy

I was in my first day of class at Galvanize and looked down to find I’d only remembered to bring my tote bag from Melville House—“I WOULD PREFER NOT TO.” How was that going to fit the new computer?

Years passed and I moved up the corporate ladder in computing.

One day I learned that a beloved world figure had died. (Perhaps a political figure with broad popular appeal). I learned this in the two chambers.

Both chambers were roughly circular, and the walls were formed by tall white linen curtains. The first room was quite small, and there was no illumination except the natural evening light, so the curtains looked soft blue. In this room I could use my laptop, so I read about the death of the leader and the widespread demonstrations of public grief.

The second chamber scared me somewhat. It was the same design as the first but three times the size, with a bright golden light streaming in from behind the curtains. In this room I could hear the gentle mechanical sounds of supercomputers. This was where one of the top 'sentient' learning machines was installed.

In this second chamber I discovered the machine was aware of the leader's death. It was also aware of the massive outpouring of grief online. Its response to these things was to start building a mechanism for itself to process the grief. It was convinced that it could take over the grieving process from the people and free them to return to their other concerns.

(Then I woke up.)
